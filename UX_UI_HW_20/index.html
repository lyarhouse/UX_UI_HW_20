Mobile App Group Project

This group project involves an app that focuses on self-care because daily living is stressful in itself. We wanted to create a space for people to go and be able to unwind and relax based on their needs.

View Project Google Drive
Research

As UX researchers, we wanted to gain a deeper understanding of the relationship between self-care and stress, in addition to learning about the causes of stress. We were also interested in understanding the tools people use to reduce stress.

Definition

ZEN was created to assist individuals with achieving self-care in a more efficient way. ZEN knows it can be difficult managing day-to-day stresses on your own, so we have made it easier for you to find what works best with your lifestyle.

Ideation

Through our user interviews, we found that while most users experienced high levels of stress related to their jobs, most users attempted to set aside dedicated time for self-care. Users want a self-care app that suggests activities because they have busy schedules that keep them from researching on their own.

Prototyping

After meaningful collaboration throughout the research, definition, and ideation stages, the group focused on prototyping a personalized onboarding process, tutorials provided with self-care activities, and an option turn on and view daily reminders for accountability.

Testing

After much testing, prototyping and iterating, we came up with the finalized features: istinction between adding an activity and scheduling to calendar, design adjustments on onboarding screens for better accessibility, a progress bar visible during onboarding quiz, and general streamlining and cleaning up of any visual issues within screens.

Conclusion

If time allowed, we would have liked to have added a feature with music since that was a highly used activity according to our initial survey data. Additionally, we could have had the opportunity to refine screens to a higher level of fidelity (adhering to Appleâ€™s Human User Guidelines).
